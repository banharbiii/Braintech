What is BrainCo?

BrainCo is a leading brain-computer interface (BCI) technology company developing non-invasive, wearable devices that decode human brain signals to create seamless interactions between humans and machines.

Our innovative solutions include:

  • FocusEDU: AI-powered classroom attention tracking system
  • FocusFit: Neurofeedback training for cognitive performance
  • Prosthetic Control: Mind-controlled robotic limbs
  • BrainOS: Platform for BCI application development

Founded by a team of Harvard innovators, BrainCo has won numerous awards including the Edison Awards Gold Medal and CES Innovation Awards.

How does it Work?

The BrainCo system measuring and interpreting brainwave signals

The BrainCo system operates through a sophisticated four-step process:

  1. Signal Detection: Wearable headbands with dry EEG sensors detect electrical activity from the user's brain
  2. Data Transmission: Brainwave signals are wirelessly sent to a processing unit in real-time
  3. AI Analysis: Proprietary algorithms filter noise and interpret cognitive states (attention, relaxation, etc.)
  4. Application Output: Processed data enables various functions - from classroom analytics to prosthetic control

This entire process happens in milliseconds, creating a seamless brain-computer interface. The system adapts to individual users over time, improving accuracy through machine learning that recognizes personal brainwave patterns.

When was it Introduced?

BrainCo's journey and product milestones:

  • 2015: Company founded by Harvard innovation team
  • 2016: First prototype developed and tested
  • 2017: FocusEDU launched in pilot schools
  • 2018: BrainRobotics prosthetic hand unveiled
  • 2019: Commercial launch of FocusFit system
  • 2020: BrainOS platform released to developers

The technology has evolved through multiple generations since its introduction, with each version bringing improved accuracy, comfort, and capabilities. BrainCo's innovations have been recognized with numerous awards including the Edison Award and CES Innovation Honors.
